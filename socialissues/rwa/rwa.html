What is an RWA here?

In Uttar Pradesh, an RWA is registered as a Society under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. It is a voluntary association of residents formed to manage common affairs. It is not a statutory government body but has legal standing to represent residents.

The "Adabi" (Abadi) Land Context

Context: Your colony is on Abadi land (old village settlement area). This differs from GNIDA-allotted sector land.

  • Registration Eligibility: You CAN form an RWA. The Registrar of Societies requires address proof (Registry/Sale Deed), which you possess. The land classification does not bar you from forming a welfare society.
  • GNIDA Role: Unlike Sector RWAs, GNIDA does not typically hand over maintenance officially to Abadi RWAs immediately. However, an RWA gives you a collective voice to petition GNIDA for connecting main roads, sewer lines, and water supply.
  • Property Title: RWA registration does not validate land title; your Sale Deed (Registry) does that. The RWA manages "Common Interest," not ownership disputes.
